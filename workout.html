What is a workout split?

A workout split is a term used in fitness to describe how an individual organizes their training sessions throughout the week. It refers to the practice of dividing a workout routine into specific training sessions, targeting different muscle groups or body parts on different days of the week.

The goal of a workout split is to allow for proper recovery time for the muscle groups. The specific workout split that works best for an individual may depend on their goals, fitness level, and personal preferences.

Full Body Split

In a full-body split, the entire body is trained in each workout session, usually 2-3 times per week. This type of workout is ideal for beginners or those who have limited time to workout. It allows for a balanced training of all muscle groups and can be an effective way to improve overall strength and fitness.

The goal of a workout split is to allow for proper recovery time for the muscle groups targeted during each training session, prevent overtraining, and maximize gains in strength, muscle mass, and overall fitness. The specific workout split that works best for an individual may depend on their goals, fitness level, and personal preferences.

Upper/Lower Split

An upper/lower split is a workout routine that splits your training sessions into two separate days: one for your upper body and one for your lower body. This is a popular training split for building strength and muscle while also allowing for adequate rest and recovery between workouts.

The key to an effective upper/lower split is to include compound exercises that target multiple muscle groups. For example, bench press and overhead press work your chest, shoulders, and triceps, while squats and lunges target your quads, hamstrings, and glutes. You should also vary your exercises and rep ranges over time to avoid plateauing and to challenge your muscles in new ways.

Upper Body Day:
  • Bench press
  • Overhead Press
  • Bent-over Rows
  • Pull Ups
  • Bicep Curls
  • Tricep Pushdowns
Lower Body Day:
  • Squats
  • Deadlifts
  • Lunges
  • Leg Press
  • Calf-Raises
  • Leg Curls

Push/Pull Split

Push/pull split is a type of weightlifting routine that divides exercises into two categories: push exercises and pull exercises. In general, push exercises work the muscles that are involved in pushing movements, such as the chest, shoulders, and triceps, while pull exercises work the muscles that are involved in pulling movements, such as the back and biceps.

The idea behind this type of split is that it allows you to focus on one set of muscles at a time, giving them time to recover between workouts. This can help prevent overuse injuries and allow you to lift heavier weights and achieve greater gains over time.

Day 1:
  • Barbell Bench press
  • Dumbell Shoulder Press
  • Tricep Dips
  • Leg Press
Day 2:
  • Pull Ups
  • Barbell Rows
  • Bicep Curls
  • Deadlifts

3 Day Split

A 3-day split is a type of weightlifting routine that divides exercises into three categories, typically by muscle group or movement pattern. The goal is to work a specific set of muscles or movement patterns on each day, allowing for optimal recovery time and muscle growth.

This split allows you to focus on different muscle groups each day while still providing adequate rest and recovery time. The exercises and rep ranges can be adjusted depending on your goals and fitness level.

Day 1: Chest + Shoulders + Triceps
  • Barbell Bench press
  • Dumbell Shoulder Press
  • Dumbbell flyes
  • Triceps pushdowns
Day 2: Back + Biceps
  • Pull Ups
  • Barbell Rows
  • Bicep Curls
  • Hammer curls
Day 3: Legs + Abs
  • Squats
  • Deadlifts
  • Leg press
  • Calf raises
  • Planks

4 Day Split

A 4-day split is a type of weightlifting routine that divides exercises into four categories, typically by muscle group or movement pattern. This allows you to focus on a specific set of muscles or movement patterns on each day while still providing adequate rest and recovery time.
